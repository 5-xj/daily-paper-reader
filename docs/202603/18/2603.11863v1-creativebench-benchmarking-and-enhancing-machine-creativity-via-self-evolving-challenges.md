## 摘要
高质量预训练数据的饱和已将研究重点转向能够持续生成新颖人工制品的演化系统，并促成了 AlphaEvolve 的成功。然而，由于缺乏严格的定量评估，此类系统的进展受到了阻碍。为了应对这一挑战，我们推出了 CreativeBench，这是一个基于经典认知框架、用于评估代码生成中机器创造力的基准测试。该基准包含两个子集——CreativeBench-Combo 和 CreativeBench-Explore，通过利用逆向工程和自我博弈（self-play）的自动化流水线，分别针对组合型创造力和探索型创造力。通过利用可执行代码，CreativeBench 使用一个定义为质量与新颖性乘积的统一指标，客观地将创造力与幻觉区分开来。我们对最先进模型的分析揭示了不同的行为特征：(1) 规模扩展（scaling）显著提高了组合型创造力，但在探索型创造力上的收益递减；(2) 更大的模型表现出“规模收敛”现象，即变得更正确但发散性更低；(3) 推理能力主要有益于受限的探索，而非组合。最后，我们提出了 EvoRePE，这是一种即插即用的推理时引导策略，它内化了演化搜索模式，从而持续增强机器创造力。

## Abstract
The saturation of high-quality pre-training data has shifted research focus toward evolutionary systems capable of continuously generating novel artifacts, leading to the success of AlphaEvolve. However, the progress of such systems is hindered by the lack of rigorous, quantitative evaluation. To tackle this challenge, we introduce CreativeBench, a benchmark for evaluating machine creativity in code generation, grounded in a classical cognitive framework. Comprising two subsets -- CreativeBench-Combo and CreativeBench-Explore -- the benchmark targets combinatorial and exploratory creativity through an automated pipeline utilizing reverse engineering and self-play. By leveraging executable code, CreativeBench objectively distinguishes creativity from hallucination via a unified metric defined as the product of quality and novelty. Our analysis of state-of-the-art models reveals distinct behaviors: (1) scaling significantly improves combinatorial creativity but yields diminishing returns for exploration; (2) larger models exhibit ``convergence-by-scaling,'' becoming more correct but less divergent; and (3) reasoning capabilities primarily benefit constrained exploration rather than combination. Finally, we propose EvoRePE, a plug-and-play inference-time steering strategy that internalizes evolutionary search patterns to consistently enhance machine creativity.
