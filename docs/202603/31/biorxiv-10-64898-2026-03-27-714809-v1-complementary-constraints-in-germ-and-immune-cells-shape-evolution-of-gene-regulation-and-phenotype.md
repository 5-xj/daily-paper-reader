## 摘要
新突变产生于生殖细胞中，必须在改变体细胞表型以赋予适应性优势的同时保持生育能力。为了理解整个生物体如何平衡这些约束，我们培育了在 Traf6 位点具有人源化非编码启动子序列的小鼠；Traf6 是 NF-kB 通路的一个组分，其在小鼠生殖细胞中的染色质状态近期发生了分化。人源化等位基因 (Traf6h) 恢复了生殖细胞和免疫细胞中类人的表达，我们将其追溯到一个削弱 CTCF 结合的 13 个碱基对缺失。Traf6h 对生育能力没有影响，但诱导了对内毒素更敏感的先天免疫反应。其他 NF-kB 通路基因也遵循类似的模式，表明它们的表达是协同演化的，这与已知的小鼠和人类在炎症反应方面的差异一致。我们的研究结果揭示了生殖细胞和免疫细胞在塑造基因调控演化中的不同作用：免疫细胞迅速调整表型以实现选择，而生殖细胞尽管表达水平发生变化，仍能稳健地维持生育能力以实现遗传。

## Abstract
New mutations arise in germ cells and must preserve fertility while altering somatic phenotype to confer a fitness advantage. To understand how whole organisms balance these constraints, we generated mice with humanized noncoding promoter sequence at Traf6, an NF-kB pathway component with recently diverged chromatin state in mouse germ cells. The humanized allele (Traf6h) reinstated human-like expression in germ and immune cells, which we traced to a 13 base pair deletion that impairs CTCF binding. Traf6h had no effect on fertility but induced a sensitized innate immune response to endotoxin. Other NF-kB pathway genes followed a similar pattern, suggesting that their expression has evolved in tandem and consistent with known mouse-human differences in inflammatory response. Our results reveal distinct roles for germ and immune cells in shaping gene regulatory evolution, where immune cells quickly adjust phenotype, enabling selection, while germ cells robustly maintain fertility despite changing expression levels, enabling inheritance.
