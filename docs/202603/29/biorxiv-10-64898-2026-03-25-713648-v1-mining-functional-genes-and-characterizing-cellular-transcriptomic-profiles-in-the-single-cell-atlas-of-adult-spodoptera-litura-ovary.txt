1.  Abstract 16 

Understanding the reproductive biology of non -model organisms  remains 17 

challenging due to the limited availability of high -resolution molecular resources. 18 

Here,  we present a comprehensive  single -cell transcriptomic atlas of the adult ovary 19 

of  Spodoptera litura  (S. litura ), a high ly polyphagous agricultural pest with a 20 

polytrophic meroistuc ovary . By integrating single -cell RNA sequencing with cross -21 

species comparison to  Drosophila melanogas ter  (D. melanogaster ), we define major 22 

germline and somatic cell populations and delineate conserved and species -specific 23 

features of ovarian cell composition. To enhance the interpretability and reuse of this 24 

dataset, we combine transcriptomic profiling w ith  in situ  hybridization to validate 25 

cluster -specific molecular markers across ovarian cell types.  We further apply RNA 26 

interference (RNAi)  to assess the contributions of germline -enriched genes (Hsc70 -4, 27 

Wech, Polo, Path) to ovarian development and fecun dity. Trajectory inference, 28 

together with SCENIC and CellChat analyses, provides a system -level view of 29 

transcriptional regulatory programs and predicted intercellular communication 30 

pathways during oogenesis in  S. litura . Collectively, this work establishe s a valuable 31 

resource for studying lepidopteran insect oogenesis, offering a comparative 32 

framework for reproductive biology in non -model insects and highlighting potential 33 

targets for RNAi -based pest control strategies. 34

2.  Introduction 37 

The tobacco cutworm,  Spodoptera litura  (Lepidoptera, Noctuidae ), is a highly 38 

destructive pest that threatens crops worldwide (1), including cotton, corn, and 39 

vegetables (2). Its remarkable reproductive capacity, with females laying thousands of 40 

eggs during their short lifespan, contributes to rapid population growth  and persistent 41 

infestations, exacerbating the challenge of pest control (3, 4). Despite extensive 42 

studies on the behavior and ecological effects of this pest, the molecular mechanisms 43 

regulating its reproductive development remain poorly understood.  As the  crucial 44 

biological process  for their robust reproductive capacity,  oogenesis  in adult  S. litura 45 

occur s in a polytrophic meroistic ovary, where germline cells interact with somatic 46 

cells in a complex and tightly regulated manner  through three critical stag es: 47 

previtellogenesis, vitellogenesis, and choriogenesis  (5) . Similar to the anatomical 48 

arrangement in  Pieris napi , Bombyx mori  and  Drosophila melanogaster  (D. 49 

melanogaster ) ovaries, the oocytes  of  S. litura  are connected closely to adjacent 50 

supporting nurse cells by cytoplasmic junctions . Through a  ser ies of precise and 51 

intricate physiological progress es , the oocytes ultimately develop into mature eggs 52 

and awaits fertilization  (6 -8). 53 

Extensive studies demo nstrate that  S. litura  has  evolved  marked resistance to 54 

most conventional synthetic  pes ticides  such as  indoxacarb , neon icotinoid , diamide 55 

and  even some  Bacillus thuringiensis  toxins  (9 -12) . Apart from this, the widespread 56 

application of these  synthetic  che mical s often nonselectively eliminate beneficial 57 

natural enemies, thereby  aggrava ting ecological imbalances  as well, which makes it 58 

an urgent issue to develop efficient and environmentally -friendly strategies for pest 59 

management.  In recent years, RNA inter ference (RNAi) -based pest management has 60 

emerged as a promising alternative  (13 -15) . Researchers have employed double -61 

stranded RNA (dsRNA) targeting reproduction -related genes to disrupt  growth and 62 

development  of reproductive system in pests . The spray of  nanocarrier -delivered 63 

dsRNA of conserved e cdysone  receptor  and juvenile hormone receptor components 64 

exhibited effective inhibition on oviposition and ovarian development in the 65

have focused on the integration of transcriptomics and RNAi to identify specific 69

Traditional approaches to study insect ovarian development have largely relied 75 

on morphological analysis and histological  methods, which provide limited insight 76 

into the dynamic cellular processes of tissues formation. The  advent  of  scRNA -seq 77 

has revolutionized our understanding of cellular heterogeneity, cell states and cell -cell 78 

communication in diverse  organism  tissues at  single -cell resolution (21 -23). 79 

Nevertheless, no studies have reported the transcriptomic signatures of distinct cell 80 

types in  S. litura  ovary. In this study, we present a single -cell transcriptomic atlas of  S. 81 

litura  ovary, providing an unprecedented view of its cellular diversity and 82 

transcriptional landscapes. By integrating scRNA -seq with  in situ  hybridization and 83

and validate their functional roles. Through a cross -species comparison with 85 

Drosophila melanogaster , we highlight conserved and unique features of ovarian cell 86 

types and developmental trajectories. Our study also explores the transcriptional 87 

regulatory networks and intercell ular communication pathways that regulate 88 

germline -somatic interactions in ovary. This work provides a pivotal resource for 89 

uncovering single -cell transcriptomic characteristics of  S. litura  ovary and elucidates 90 

unique gene regulatory networks in non -model  insects. 91

5.  Discussion 410 

Insect ovaries provide the cellular and molecular foundation for germline 411 

maintenance and reproductive output, yet our understanding of ovarian cell -type 412 

organization and regulatory programs remains heavily biased toward a  small number 413 

of model species. Although single -cell transcriptomics has transfromed the analysis of 414 

cellular heterogeneity in diverse tissues, its application to non -model insects has been 415 

constrained by limited reference atlases and poorly defined cell -type markers (27 -29). 416 

In this study, we establish a single -cell transcriptomic resource for  S. litura  ovary, 417 

enabling systematic dissection of ovarian cell states and their transcriptional features 418 

at single -cell resolution. 419 

Drosophila  and Many Lepidoptera insects such as  Pieris napi , Bombyx mori  and 420 

S. litura  have polytrophic meroistic ovary, suggesting a tissue -specific revolutionary 421 

conservation between these two species (6 -8). Leveraging the conserved polytrophic 422 

meroistic architect ure shared between  D. melanogaster  and  S. litura , we  implemented 423 

a cross -species orthology -based annotation strategy to map 14 transcriptionally 424 

distinct clusters onto 5 predicted cell types and to integrate these findings into a 425 

schematic model of the adu lt  S. litura  ovariole (Figure 2A). Compared with traditional 426 

histological and antibody -based approaches (30 -32), our single -cell framework 427 

resolves ovarian cellular complexity with substantially higher resolution, identifying 9 428 

somatic cell clusters and 4  germline cell clusters in  S. litura  ovary and provideing 429 

molecular markers for each cell type. Spatial validation by  in situ  hybridization 430

undifferentiated germ cell s were  def ined  by  cycB  expression , however,  putative 432

droplet -based single -cell sequencing. These obse rvations highlight a general 435 

challenge for ovarian single -cell studies and underscore the need for future 436 

enrichment approaches to capture rare germ cell populations. 437 

It is reported that  S. litura  has developed to strong adaptability and resistance to 438 

comm ercial pesticides, and genetically evolved  multi -copy detoxification genes  in its 439 

genome (1, 33). In contrast to the application of conventional pesticides, RNAi -based 440 

pest  management  is a promising strategy to control agricultural pests with high 441 

efficiency and low toxicity to non -targeted organisms (34 -36).  However, the identity 442 

and functional relevance of reproduction -associated genes in  S. litura  remain poorly 443 

characterized, limiti ng the rational selection of effective RNAi targets. Functional 444 

interrogation of germline -specific  marker genes using RNAi technique demonstrates 445 

that knock down of  Hsc70 -4, Wech , PPn , Polo  and  Path  markedly impairs ovarian 446 

development and reduces fecundit y (Figures 5C -5F), indicating that our single -cell 447 

atlas constitutes a valuable reference for systematically prioritizing novel reproductive 448 

targets in  S. litura  and other lepidopteran pests. 449 

Beyond cell -type annotation, this resouce enables comparative an d functional 450 

analyses of ovarian regulatory programs. Pathway enrichment analyses reveal that 451 

germ cells are characterized by transcriptional programs associated with cancer 452 

disease, carbohydrate metabolism, cellular transport  and  catabolism and DNA 453 

replic ation and repair were enriched in germ cells, mirroring features in previously 454 

described  Drosophila  germ cells (25, 37). Moreover, one regulon E2F2_extended that 455 

has been identified as an active gene regulatory network in undifferentiated germ cell, 456 

older  germ cell and escort cells in  Drosophila  ovary also exhibited specific activity in 457 

undifferentiated germ cell, oocyte, early MBFC and early somatic cells in  S. litura 458 

ovary, supporting the evolutionary conservation of key regulatory principles 459

6. Limitations of the study 465 

Our present work  identified a useful atlas of  single -cell  S. litura  ovary 466 

transcriptomics  combin g with SCENIC and CellChat analys is,  which  provid es 467 

essential  resources for  grasping  the heterogeneity and  cellular  regulatory networks  in 468 

S. litura  ovar y. Despite the comprehensive dataset generated in this study, one 469 

limitation is the difficulty in capturing GSCs, which are scarce in ovary  tissues. 470 

Meanwhile, some  limitations including sample size  and  sequencing depth  may hinder 471 

a comprehensive understanding of complex intercellular interactions  in  S. litura  ova ry .472 

Future  studies  should  focus on developing improved techniques for GSCs enric hment 473 

or combining with more refined cell isolation methods such as fluorescence -activated 474 

cell sorting (FACS) or microfluidic -based approaches. Moreover,  integrat ing  multi -475 

omics  technologies  such as proteomics, epigenomics, and spatial transcriptomics 476 

cou ld offer a more holistic understanding of gene regulation and cellular interactions 477 

in  S. litura  ova ries .478
