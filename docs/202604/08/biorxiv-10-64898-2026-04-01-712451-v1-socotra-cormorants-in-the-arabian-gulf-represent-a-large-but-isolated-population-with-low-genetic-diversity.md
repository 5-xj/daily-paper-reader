## 摘要
索科特拉鸬鹚（Phalacrocorax nigrogularis）是一种受威胁的海鸟，特有于阿拉伯湾和阿拉伯海沿岸地区，这两个区域由霍尔木兹海峡分隔。保护受威胁物种需要明确种群边界并评估遗传多样性。然而，有关索科特拉鸬鹚种群结构和遗传变异的信息仍然缺乏，这些信息对于此类评估至关重要。在本研究中，我们利用两种不同的遗传标记评估了索科特拉鸬鹚的种群结构和遗传多样性：（1）母系遗传的线粒体DNA细胞色素氧化酶1（COI）和（2）核非编码区段β-纤维蛋白原内含子7（FIB7）。共采集了279个个体，来自阿拉伯湾的四个繁殖群落以及阿拉伯海哈西基亚岛的一个群落。基于COI变异的结果显示，阿拉伯湾的群落构成一个具有广泛基因流动的大型种群——除了最远的一对群落之外——但与阿拉伯海的哈西基亚群落隔离。COI变异显示湾内群落与哈西基亚群落之间存在显著分化，这与已报道的分布模式一致，可能反映了该地区的系统地理过程。湾内种群的COI多样性显著较低，其核苷酸多样性和单倍型多样性均显著低于哈西基亚。相比之下，FIB7结果显示群落间存在广泛的连通性，没有可检测到的种群结构或湾内种群与哈西基亚之间的显著差异。本研究首次描述了索科特拉鸬鹚的种群结构和遗传多样性。由于阿拉伯湾索科特拉鸬鹚的遗传多样性较低且种群相对孤立，这引发了关于其长期生存力的保护忧虑，因为这可能会降低其适应能力并削弱其应对环境变化的进化潜力。

通俗摘要：索科特拉鸬鹚是一种分布于阿拉伯湾和阿拉伯海的受威胁海鸟，但此前对其种群结构和遗传多样性的了解甚少。我们利用两个遗传标记分析了来自五个繁殖群落（湾内四个，海上一个）的279只鸟，以评估种群连通性和遗传变异。研究发现，阿拉伯湾内的索科特拉鸬鹚似乎形成一个庞大但遗传上相对孤立且遗传多样性较低的种群。这是首次评估该濒危海鸟种群结构和遗传多样性的研究。这些发现对阿拉伯湾索科特拉鸬鹚的保护具有重要意义，因为遗传多样性低及相对孤立与适应力下降相关，表明其适应环境变化的能力可能较弱。

## Abstract
The Socotra Cormorant (Phalacrocorax nigrogularis) is a threatened seabird endemic to the coastal areas of the Arabian Gulf and the Arabian Sea, two regions separated by the Strait of Hormuz. Conserving threatened species requires clear delineation of population boundaries and the evaluation of genetic diversity. However, information on population structure and genetic variation, necessary for such an assessment, is lacking for the Socotra Cormorants. In this study, we assessed population structure and genetic diversity of Socotra Cormorants using two contrasting genetic markers: (1) maternally inherited mtDNA cytochrome oxidase 1 (COI) and (2) a nuclear non-coding region, {beta}-fibrinogen intron 7 (FIB7). A total of 279 individuals were sampled from four colonies in the Arabian Gulf and one colony on Hasikiyah Island in the Arabian Sea. Findings based on COI-variation suggest that the Arabian Gulf colonies represent one large population with extensive gene flow between Gulf colonies--except for the most distant pair of colonies--but isolated from Hasikiyah in the Arabian Sea. COI-variation indicated significant differentiation between the colonies inside the Gulf and the Hasikiyah colony. This is consistent with the reported distribution patterns, and may reflect phylogeographic processes of the region. The Gulf population showed substantially lower COI-diversity, with significantly lower nucleotide and haplotype diversity compared to Hasikiyah. In contrast, FIB7 results indicated extensive connectivity among colonies, with no detectable population structure or significant differences between the Gulf population and Hasikiyah. This study presents the first characterization of population structure and genetic diversity of Socotra Cormorants. The low genetic diversity coupled with relative isolation of the Gulf Socotra Cormorants raises conservation concerns regarding their long-term viability by potentially reducing fitness and eroding their evolutionary capacity to adapt to environmental change.
