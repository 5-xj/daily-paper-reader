## 摘要
分子内上位性（intramolecular epistasis）日益被认为是决定蛋白质不同位点间进化速率变化模式并限制适应性进化的重要因素。尽管基因组范围的分析揭示了分子内上位性相互作用能够驱动氨基酸替换的空间聚集，但此类相互作用及其进化后果的直接实证证据仍然有限。通过在人群遗传层面筛选果蝇蛋白中空间聚集且谱系特异性的适应性氨基酸替换，我们系统地确定了可用于功能分析的实验候选对象。作为概念验证，我们聚焦于 Trio 蛋白——一种 Rho 鸟嘌呤核苷酸交换因子，该蛋白在黑腹果蝇（D. melanogaster）谱系中表现出三个空间聚集的推测性适应性氨基酸替换。通过利用基因组编辑在体内系统性重构进化中间体，我们发现所有可能的中间状态都表现出存活率降低和/或运动缺陷，提供了强有力的证据证明上位性对进化轨迹具有约束作用。值得注意的是，这些有害效应是隐性的，表明上位性相互作用的氨基酸替换的中间组合可以在杂合体中积累，随后在固定前规避由不适应性中间状态施加的明显约束。这些发现共同提供了一个受分子内上位性塑造的适应性景观的罕见实证视角，并建立了在二倍体多细胞生物中研究蛋白质适应性进化约束的框架。

作者概要：蛋白质折叠成三维结构，这一结构对其功能至关重要。由于这种结构依赖于氨基酸之间的相互作用，一个位点上的突变所产生的适应性效应可能取决于其他位点的氨基酸状态。这类依赖关系限制了蛋白质在中性或适应性进化过程中可能采取的路径。尽管分子内上位性在微生物系统和体外实验中已有所证明，但在二倍体多细胞生物体内直接的实验证据却十分罕见。本文识别出了一些果蝇蛋白，它们具有紧密聚集的推测性适应性氨基酸替换簇，并将实验分析的重点放在一个例子——Trio（一种 Rho 鸟嘌呤核苷酸交换因子）上。通过基因组编辑重构黑腹果蝇谱系中的进化中间体，我们发现所有 Trio 的中间版本都会降低存活率或损害运动功能。重要的是，这些有害效应是隐性的，说明它们在与祖先版本蛋白配对时可能被掩盖。这意味着单个氨基酸改变可能在种群中的杂合体中长期存在，随后结合起来促进适应。如果沿着适应性进化路径的有害中间体普遍呈隐性，这可能对我们理解蛋白质适应性进化的机制产生重要影响。

## Abstract
Intramolecular epistasis is increasingly recognized as a key factor shaping patterns of evolutionary rate variation among protein sites and constraining adaptive evolution. While genome-wide analyses have revealed that intramolecular epistatic interactions can drive the spatial clustering of amino acid substitutions, direct empirical evidence for such interactions and their evolutionary consequences remains limited. Using a population genetic screen for spatially-clustered and lineage-specific adaptive amino acid substitutions in Drosophila proteins, we systematically identify experimentally tractable candidates for functional analysis. As proof of concept, we focus on the Trio protein, a Rho guanine nucleotide exchange factor that exhibits three spatially-clustered putatively adaptive amino acid substitutions in the D. melanogaster lineage. By systematically reconstructing evolutionary intermediates in vivo using genome editing, we find that all possible intermediate states exhibit reduced viability and/or locomotor defects, providing strong evidence for epistatic constraints on evolutionary trajectories. Notably, these deleterious effects are recessive, suggesting that intermediate combinations of epistatically interacting amino acid substitutions can accumulate in heterozygotes prior to fixation, thereby circumventing apparent constraints imposed by maladaptive intermediate states. Together, these findings provide a rare empirical view of the fitness landscape shaped by intramolecular epistasis and establish a framework for investigating the constraints on adaptive protein evolution in diploid multicellular organisms.

Author SummaryProteins fold into three-dimensional structures that are essential for their function. Because these structures depend on interactions among amino acids, the fitness effect of a mutation at one site can depend on the amino acid states at other sites. Such dependencies constrain the paths that protein evolution can take, whether evolution proceeds neutrally or adaptively. Although intramolecular epistasis has been demonstrated in microbial systems and in vitro, direct experimental evidence for such constraints in diploid multicellular organisms in vivo is rare. Here, we identify Drosophila proteins that exhibit clusters of closely-spaced putatively adaptive amino acid substitutions and focus experimental analyses on one example, Trio, a Rho guanine nucleotide exchange factor. Using genome editing to reconstruct evolutionary intermediates in the D. melanogaster lineage, we find that all intermediate versions of Trio reduce viability or impair locomotor function. Importantly, these harmful effects are recessive, suggesting that they could be masked when paired with an ancestral version of the protein. This implies that individual amino acid changes may persist in heterozygotes within populations and later combine to contribute to adaptation. If the recessivity of deleterious intermediates along adaptive evolutionary paths proves to be widespread, it could have important implications for our mechanistic understanding of adaptive protein evolution.
