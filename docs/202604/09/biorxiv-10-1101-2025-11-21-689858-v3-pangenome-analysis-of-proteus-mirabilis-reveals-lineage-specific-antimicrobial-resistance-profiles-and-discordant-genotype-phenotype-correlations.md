## 摘要
泌尿道感染（UTI）带来了巨大的医疗负担，并呈现抗菌药物耐药性（AMR）和治疗失败不断加剧的趋势。由于具有内在耐药性和生物膜形成能力，变形杆菌（Proteus mirabilis）是一种尚未充分表征且具有挑战性的泌尿道感染病原体。为了解 P. mirabilis 的群体基因组学特征，我们结合了泛基因组分析、计算机预测的抗菌药物耐药性（AMR）以及基于表型的药物敏感性测试（AST），分析了 1,027 株来源于人体尿液样本的 P. mirabilis 基因组。结果显示，P. mirabilis 具有由高度可塑的附属基因组驱动的镶嵌式泛基因组结构。多位点序列分型（MLST）共识别出 213 个 MLST 型，其中仅有 7% 的类型包含 10 个或更多基因组，显示出高度的菌株多样性。AMR 基因谱在很大程度上与谱系相关，约 25% 的基因组携带超过 6 个抗菌药物亚类的耐药基因。ST135 被鉴定为一种高度多重耐药谱系，其 95% 的基因组含有 16 个或更多耐药基因。对 22 株具有完整、参考级基因组的临床分离株进行可移动遗传元件（MGE）分析发现，Tn7 转座子、由 IS26 介导的基因岛以及 I 类整合子是高水平耐药基因传播的载体，其中包括在 ST135 株中通过 IS26 介导在变形杆菌耐药岛 1（PmGRI1）内发生的耐药基因堆叠。尽管如 aph(3')-Ia 等基因的存在能够可靠预测卡那霉素耐药性，但对于甲氧苄啶-磺胺甲噁唑和氯霉素等抗生素则存在不一致关系，揭示了耐药基因堆叠、调控环境以及如外排泵等内在机制会调节表型结果。总之，本研究提供了对 P. mirabilis 抗药性的全面表型解析，确立了耐药结构呈谱系分化、由可移动遗传元件驱动且在表型上不具决定性。我们强调应转向标准化、基于基因组信息的监测框架，以便用于诊断和治疗策略。

## Abstract
Urinary tract infections (UTIs) impose a large healthcare burden, with escalating antimicrobial resistance (AMR) and treatment failure. Proteus mirabilis is an under-characterized and challenging UTI pathogen due to intrinsic resistances and biofilm formation. To understand P. mirabilis population genomics, we combined pangenome analysis, in silico AMR predication, and phenotypic antimicrobial susceptibility testing (AST) across 1,027 P. mirabilis genomes derived from human urine specimens. This revealed a mosaic pangenome driven by extensive accessory genome plasticity. Multilocus sequence typing (MLST) identified 213 MLSTs with only 7% having 10 or more genomes, highlighting strain diversity. AMR gene profiles were largely lineage specific, with 25% of genomes harboring resistances for >6 antimicrobial subclasses. ST135 was identified as a highly MDR lineage, with 95% of genomes carrying 16 or more resistance genes. Mobile genetic element (MGE) analysis of 22 clinical isolates with complete, reference level genomes revealed that Tn7 transposons, IS26-mediated genomic islands, and class 1 integrons act as vehicles for high AMR gene dissemination, including IS26-mediated gene stacking within a Proteus mirabilis Genomic Resistance Island 1 (PmGRI1) in ST135 isolates. While presence of genes like aph(3')-la reliably predicted kanamycin resistance, discordance for antibiotics such as trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole and chloramphenicol revealed that AMR gene stacking, regulatory context, and intrinsic mechanisms, like efflux pumps, modulate phenotypic outcomes. In summary, our study provides a comprehensive and phenotypic resolution of P. mirabilis AMR, establishing that resistance architecture is lineage structured, MGE-driven, and phenotypically non-deterministic. We emphasize the need to shift towards standardized, genome-informed surveillance framework to translate into diagnostic and therapeutic strategies.
